[해설]

a-\sin ^{3} \theta=\cos ^{3} \theta
\end{array}$$

$$\begin{array}{l}
\sin ^{2} \theta+2 \sin \theta \cos \theta+\cos ^{2} \theta=\frac{1}{4} \\
1+2 \sin \theta \cos \theta=\frac{1}{4} \quad \therefore \sin \theta \cos \theta=-\frac{3}{8}
\end{array}$$

$$\begin{aligned}
a & =\sin ^{3} \theta+\cos ^{3} \theta \\

& =\frac{1}{2} \cdot\left(1+\frac{3}{8}\right)=\frac{11}{16}
\end{aligned}$$
